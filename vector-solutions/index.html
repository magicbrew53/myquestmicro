The context

Fifteen hundred industrial courses. Learning paths that end at supervisor. One missing layer.

While supervisors feel accountable for both safety and culture, many say they lack the preparation and resources to deliver on those responsibilities.

— Vector Solutions, The State of Frontline Safety Leadership in Construction research (2025)

Vector Solutions is how critical industries stay trained — course libraries, LMS, and EHS software embedded in the daily workflows of more than twenty-four thousand organizations, with an Industrial Premium Library built for manufacturers, maintenance teams, frontline workers, and supervisors.

The leadership pathway exists too. Vector's own learning paths combine industrial skills, safety, and leadership courses to carry a worker from entry-level technician to senior technician to supervisor, with competency assessments and reporting along the way. The content is sound.

What a completed pathway can't produce is skill. A technician who finishes the leadership courses and passes the assessment has demonstrated knowledge. Whether they can run a hard conversation on their first shift as a supervisor — and whether their plant manager can see the difference — is a separate question. That's the layer this partnership is about: turning content into measured capability.

Where we'd start

Three themes where the skill-building layer attaches to the existing catalog.

Each theme is a place where Vector Solutions's existing content does what it does well — and where myQuest adds the measured-skill layer that turns content into proven capability.

THEME01

Skill development for the path that ends at supervisor

Vector's learning paths do something most catalogs don't: they carry a person from entry-level technician all the way to supervisor, with leadership courses built into the sequence. The last rung is the one the industry stumbles on — the day the best technician becomes responsible for people.

MQS attaches deliberate skill-building and manager observation to the leadership courses already in the path, so the path delivers a capable supervisor, not just a completed sequence.

The pitch in one line

"Your path builds the technician. We help it finish the job — building the leader."

THEME02

The conversations your own research says supervisors fear

Vector's own frontline leadership research — six hundred supervisors surveyed in construction, an adjacent frontline context — found supervisors carrying weight they weren't prepared for: nearly two-thirds worry that saying the wrong thing in a sensitive crew conversation could make things worse, and most want better guidance for the hardest moments.

Those conversations — safety interventions, distress, performance — are skills. Skills can be built deliberately, observed by a manager, and measured.

The pitch in one line

"Your research named the fear. This builds the skill that answers it."

THEME03

Readiness reporting, extended to demonstrated skill

Vector already gives plants competency assessments and readiness reporting — that's part of what 24,000 organizations buy. The natural extension is the layer assessments can't reach: not what a supervisor knows, but what they've demonstrably done, rep after rep, observed on the floor.

Per-person skill maps slot into the readiness story Vector already tells, and give plant leadership evidence at the level the word "readiness" actually implies.

The pitch in one line

"Your reports show what they know. This shows what they can do."

This is what a skill map looks like in practice. Named skills, observed proficiency, per-employee.
How it works

The four pieces of the partnership.

Your content stays your content. We add the layer that turns it into measured capability.

1

Foundation

Learners complete the existing Vector Solutions courses on the existing platform. Nothing rebuilds, nothing replaces.

2

Measured practice

After a learning moment, learners build the skill through realistic AI-driven conversation reps and reinforcement work — at scale, on their schedule.

3

Manager observation

Supervisors and managers get structured prompts that turn training content into on-the-job coaching and skill reinforcement.

4

Skill maps

Per-employee proficiency on the named skills your enterprise customers actually care about. Proficiency, not completions.
