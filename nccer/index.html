The context

The craft training standard. A leadership series to the superintendent's chair. One missing layer.

Craft professionals are often called upon and promoted to foreman without the training they need, like communication, leadership and management skills.

— NCCER, "Tips for Building an Effective Construction Foreman Training Program"

NCCER is the standard the construction industry built for itself — a not-for-profit education foundation whose curricula, assessments, and Registry credentials are how contractors, schools, and training sponsors across the country develop craft professionals. The catalog spans more than forty craft areas, from core construction skills to journey-level certification.

The leadership pathway exists too. Fundamentals of Crew Leadership moves journey-level craft workers into frontline roles; the Construction Foreman Certification runs five courses from leadership and communication through quality and safety; the Superintendent Certification is ACCE-certified. The content is sound.

What certification can't produce is skill. A foreman who passes the program assessment has demonstrated knowledge. Whether they can correct an experienced craftsman on a live jobsite — and whether their superintendent can see the difference — is a separate question. That's the layer this partnership is about: turning content into measured capability.

Where we'd start

Three themes where the skill-building layer attaches to the existing catalog.

Each theme is a place where NCCER's existing content does what it does well — and where myQuest adds the measured-skill layer that turns content into proven capability.

THEME01

Skill development for the foreman pipeline, not just certification

The Leadership Series is built to move craft professionals up — crew leader, foreman, superintendent. Each rung is a curriculum plus an assessment. The rung the industry actually stumbles on is the day-one reality: giving direction to yesterday's peers, addressing performance, resolving crew conflict — the exact skills NCCER's own materials name.

MQS attaches deliberate skill-building and manager observation to the existing courses, so the pipeline develops capability, not just completions.

The pitch in one line

"Your series teaches the principle. We help your learners build the skill — and show you they've built it."

THEME02

The jobsite conversations foremen avoid

The Safety, Health and Environment course asks foremen to build a zero-incident culture — hazard recognition, remediation, incident preparedness. The hard part isn't the checklist; it's the conversation: stopping work, correcting a 20-year craftsman, responding when a worker reports an incident.

Those are skills, and they're buildable and measurable.

The pitch in one line

"A zero-incident culture is built in conversations. We help your foremen build — and measure — that skill."

THEME03

Contractors are starting to ask what the credential can't show

The Registry is the industry's proof of knowledge — portable, verifiable, trusted. But when a contractor puts a newly certified foreman in front of a crew of eight to twelve, the question shifts from "what do they know" to "how do they lead" — and NCCER's own case studies already sell outcomes like retention.

Per-person skill maps give sponsors and contractors evidence at that level: capability, not completion.

The pitch in one line

"The Registry proves they know it. This shows contractors they can do it."

This is what a skill map looks like in practice. Named skills, observed proficiency, per-employee.
How it works

The four pieces of the partnership.

Your content stays your content. We add the layer that turns it into measured capability.

1

Foundation

Learners complete the existing NCCER courses on the existing platform. Nothing rebuilds, nothing replaces.

2

Measured practice

After a learning moment, learners build the skill through realistic AI-driven conversation reps and reinforcement work — at scale, on their schedule.

3

Manager observation

Supervisors and managers get structured prompts that turn training content into on-the-job coaching and skill reinforcement.

4

Skill maps

Per-employee proficiency on the named skills your enterprise customers actually care about. Proficiency, not completions.
